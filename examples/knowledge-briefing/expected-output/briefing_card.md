# Briefing Card: Municipal Heat Planning — Capacity and Data Gaps

## Executive Summary

German municipalities face significant capacity and data constraints in meeting the federal heat planning mandate. Smaller municipalities lack dedicated staff, building stock data is fragmented across incompatible systems, and the timeline is ambitious relative to current completion rates.

## Supported Findings

1. **Municipal capacity gap**: Municipalities under 20,000 residents typically have no dedicated energy planning staff. Three Landkreise confirmed this at the March 2026 regional workshop.

2. **Low completion rates**: As of January 2026, only 14% of municipalities above 10,000 have completed a formal heat plan, against a mid-2028 federal deadline. Approximately 60% of affected municipalities must begin planning within 12 months.

3. **Funding availability but uneven uptake**: NKI funding covers up to 90% of planning costs, but uptake varies by Bundesland.

4. **Data integration challenges**: A pilot in Landkreis Beispiel found that 15% of utility records could not be matched to cadastral parcels. Roughly 7–8% represent genuine data gaps that must be flagged rather than silently excluded.

5. **Demand for standardized tools**: Both workshop participants and municipal leaders independently called for shared assessment templates and standardized planning tools.

## Operational Implications

- Planning support should prioritize shared templates and tools over bespoke consulting, since smaller municipalities cannot absorb consultant-dependent approaches.
- Data integration pilots should include explicit gap-flagging rather than treating unmatched records as noise.
- Inter-municipal coordination through Landkreise is emerging as a practical model but adds coordination overhead that should be accounted for.

## Open Questions

- What connection rates are realistic for district heating in existing building stock? One source suggests 40–60%, but the note may predate the 2024 Building Energy Act amendments. This requires updated evidence.
- How are other Bundesländer addressing the uneven NKI funding uptake?
